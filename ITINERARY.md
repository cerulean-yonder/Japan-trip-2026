### Days 2–3 — Sunday–Monday, April 19–20 | Kamakura

Kamakura is a coastal city ~50 km south of Tokyo, known for Zen temples, hiking trails, and a relaxed seaside atmosphere. Good for acclimatising after the flight.

**Activities:**
- **Kotoku-in (高徳院)** — The famous *Daibutsu* (Great Buddha), a 13.35 m bronze statue dating from 1252. You can enter the statue for a small fee.
- **Wagashi-making** — Hands-on workshop making traditional Japanese sweets (*wagashi*) from sweet bean paste.
- **Temple visits** — Key sites: Tsurugaoka Hachimangu (main shrine), Engakuji, Hasedera.
- **Street exploration** — Komachi-dori shopping street near the station; snacks, crafts, souvenirs.

---

### Day 3 — Monday, April 20 | Kamakura → Niigata Prefecture

- Shinkansen from Tokyo to Niigata (~2.5 hrs — Joetsu Shinkansen from Tokyo Station)
- Begin the regional/rural phase of the trip

---

### Days 3–9 — April 20–26 | Niigata Region

Niigata Prefecture sits on the Sea of Japan coast, known for exceptional rice (*Koshihikari*), sake, fresh seafood, and snow country landscapes — authentic rural Japan with few tourists.

#### Tochio (栃尾)
- Small inland town in the Nagaoka city area
- Local specialty: *abura-age* (thick deep-fried tofu)

#### Teradomari (寺泊)
- Coastal fishing town on the Sea of Japan
- **Aquarium** — Niigata Prefectural Teradomari Aquarium, one of Japan's oldest; focuses on local Sea of Japan species
- **Fish market street** — Fresh seafood stalls along the waterfront; excellent crab, prawns, and seasonal catches

#### Tokamachi (十日町)
- Permanent art installations from the Echigo-Tsumari Art Triennale scattered across the landscape
- Traditional *Chijimi* silk fabric weaving heritage

#### Murakami (村上)
- **Sake tasting** — Niigata is Japan's premier sake prefecture; Murakami has local breweries worth visiting
- **Chopstick-making** — Murakami is famous for lacquerware and wooden crafts; workshops available
- Family gatherings

---

### Days 9–10 — Sunday–Monday, April 26–27 | Sanjo / Nagaoka *(Matti joins Apr 26)*

- **Grave visits** — Traditional *ohaka mairi* (grave visit/cleaning), a common and meaningful part of hometown trips in Japan
- **School tour**
- **Shopping**
- **Accommodation:** 長岡グランドホテル (*Nagaoka Grand Hotel*), 4-6-1 Saiwaicho, Nagaoka, Niigata

> **Nagaoka** is Niigata's second-largest city. Sanjo is a neighbouring city known for metalworking and hardware manufacturing.

---

### Days 11–13 — Wednesday–Friday, April 29–May 1 | Niigata City

Niigata City is the prefectural capital — a modern port city with an excellent food scene centred on seafood and rice dishes.

**Activities:**
- **Strawberry picking** at Shirone Grape Garden (白根 area, Minami Ward) — picking season runs March–May
- **Echigo Sake Museum "Ponshukan" (ぽんしゅ館)** — inside Niigata Station; 93 Niigata sake varieties available via coin-operated tasting dispensers (~¥200/taste). Also sells sake, local food, and gifts.
- **Aquarium visit** — Marinepia Nihonkai (Niigata City Aquarium)
- **University exploration** — campus visit

---

## Transport Notes

- **IC Card (Suica/Pasmo):** Get one at Narita on arrival. Works on almost all trains, buses, and at convenience stores.
- **Shinkansen:** Book Tokyo↔Niigata tickets in advance, especially around Golden Week (late April/early May is a busy travel period in Japan).
- **JR Pass:** Consider whether a JR Pass is cost-effective based on your total Shinkansen usage.
- **Golden Week warning:** Late April–early May is Japan's busiest domestic travel period. Trains, tourist sites, and restaurants will be crowded. Book accommodation and Shinkansen seats well in advance.
